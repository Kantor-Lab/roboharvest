Outdoor Autonomous Pepper Harvesting: Imitation Learning in Unstructured Agricultural Environments

Chung Hee Kim, Abhisesh Silwal, George Kantor
Carnegie Mellon University
Paper Video Code Data

Automating tasks in outdoor agricultural fields poses significant challenges due to environmental variability, unstructured terrain, and diverse crop characteristics. We present a robotic system for autonomous pepper harvesting designed to operate in these unprotected, complex settings. Utilizing a custom handheld shear-gripper, we collected 300 demonstrations to train a visuomotor policy, enabling the system to adapt to varying field conditions and crop diversity. Achieving a success rate of 28.95% with a cycle time of 31.71 seconds, our system demonstrates the feasibility and effectiveness of leveraging imitation learning for automated harvesting in unstructured agricultural environments. This work aims to advance scalable, automated robotic solutions for agriculture in natural settings.
